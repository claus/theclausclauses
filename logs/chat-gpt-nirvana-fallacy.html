What is the concept of things being so good that people avoid trying to make them bad called?

The concept you're describing is known as the "Nirvana fallacy" or "perfect solution fallacy." This fallacy occurs when people assume that if a solution is not perfect, it is not worth pursuing, or when they compare a realistic option to an idealized, perfect alternative. This can lead to inaction or the dismissal of valid solutions, as people may avoid trying to make things better for fear of making them worse. The Nirvana fallacy can be harmful because it overlooks the fact that progress often comes through incremental improvements rather than achieving a perfect state all at once.
